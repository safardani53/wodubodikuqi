All About Mark Lanegan

Lanegan, who was born on November 25, 1964, rose to prominence as the main vocalist of Screaming Trees, as well as a member of Queens of the Stone Age and the Gutter Twins. Lanegan recorded eleven studio albums, three collaborations with Isobel Campbell and two with Duke Garwood. His baritone voice made him well-known among the general population.

The Ellensburg, Washington native started his musical in 1984 as the vocalist of Screaming Trees, with whom he recorded seven studio albums and five EPs until the band disbanded in 2000. He also launched a solo career at the time, releasing The Winding Sheet, his first solo studio album, in 1990. Lanegan recorded ten additional solo albums, all of which were pretty successful.
