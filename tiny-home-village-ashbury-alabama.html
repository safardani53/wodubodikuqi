A TINY home village in Alabama has cozy apartments starting at $105 a night.

This community claims to be a paradise for certain types of guests thanks to its surrounding natural beauty.

Ashbury Tiny Homes is a small community of affordable housing in Albertville, Alabama.

What makes this village stand out is its proximity to the stunning Lake Guntersville, described as a “fisherman’s paradise” by the Airbnb listing. 

Ashbury Tiny Homes accommodates boat parking and has facilities for battery charging.

Specifically, the village caters to fishermen, traveling nurses, and corporate guests, or those looking for a more homey alternative to a hotel.

Ashbury Tiny Homes Village is made up of mostly one-bedroom, one-bath units, although there are also two, two-bedroom and two-bathroom units available.

The apartments are all designed for short-term stays.

Nightly bookings start at $105, but this rate may drop for longer stays, and prospective guests are encouraged to call to get a custom price.

Inside, the houses are equipped with a microwave, dishwasher, washer and dryer.

All furniture is provided, even down to the bedding - so guests need nothing to move in.

The community opened in September 2022, meaning the homes are all still in great condition.

FOREVER TINY HOME

While Ashbury Tiny Home Village caters to shorter-term stays, there are tiny home villages all over America available to settle down permanently. 

Over in North Carolina, Acony Bell is a 50-acre community situated among the Blue Ridge mountains.

While Acony Bell does not build or sell tiny homes, its residents rent plots of land on which they can build their unique own houses.

Residents pay around $550 a month in rent, which covers bills like water, sewage, recycling, and common area maintenance.

Homeowners can enjoy the beautiful community center and community garden with a chicken coop and edible blueberry plants.

Among the 92 residents, there are newly-wed couples, middle-aged residents, and retirees.

Another tiny home village, Tiny Tranquility, has 43 residents living in unique coastal homes in Waldport, Oregon.

And over in Detroit, Michigan, a village of 25 affordable tiny homes has been created for low-income residents thanks to donations by Jon Bon Jovi, Ford, and General Motors.
