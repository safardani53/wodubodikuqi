The East London state school rivalling UK's posh private institutions by sending 22 pupils to Oxbrid

Publish date: 2024-07-08

AN East London state school now rivals the UK's poshest private schools as 22 of its students have been offered places at either Oxford and Cambridge.

The London Academy of Excellence (LAE), known as the "Eton of the East End", was set up six years ago in Stratford, with the aim of getting smart children from poor families into top universities.

One in 10 of LAE's upper sixth pupils will head off to the prestigious universities in the summer, as long as they achieve their required grades in the summer.

This puts the free school on par with England's famous public schools including the likes of 587-year-old Eton College which charges more than £12,000 per term.

LAE Headmaster Scott Baker said: "With hard work, the aspiration to succeed, excellent teaching and the right support and guidance, there really are no limits on what young people can achieve, irrespective of their gender, ethnicity or background."

The selective free school which has around 10 applicants competing for each place, selects its pupils at 16-years-old, based on their GCSE results and interviews.

Many of its pupils are the first in their family to go to university in the UK.

Their offers came after months of hard work, with one student spending four hours a night studying after school.

Louis Baxter told the Times he can still remember his shock as he was interviewed by the historian Gary Gerstle.

He said: "He's a world-renowned historian who has made documentaries for the BBC. They asked: 'How do you think religion affects democracy?', and I remember saying: 'Ha ha, that's a good question!'"

LAE is currently backed by six private schools, including Eton, Highgate in north London, and Brighton College.

After its success, other private schools are understood to be in talks to sponsor more selective sixth forms in cities, including Birmingham and Manchester.

Mr Baker stressed the importance of having confidence in the Oxbridge interviews. He hopes Oxbridge offers will "reflect the breadth of talent there is in the country".

SOME OF THE OXBRIDGE CANDIDATES

Mirana Kqiku, 18, has received an offer to study French at Oxford's Oriel College. She is set to become the first person in her family to go to university.

Born in London after her parents moved from Kosovo, Kqiku gave up her Friday evenings to practise interviews.

Raluca Popcan hopes to study history and modern languages at Cambridge

Shakila Akhtar has been offered a place to study law at Oxford

Bartek Staniszewski hopes to study philosophy and theology after recieving his place at Oxford
