Gloucester rugby coach Johan Ackermann and star-player son Ruan accused of nightclub fight

Publish date: 2024-05-23

Gloucester coach Johan Ackermann and his star-player son Ruan have been accused of a nightclub fight which police are investigating.

The South African rugby boss and his No 8 were accused on social media by the relatives of a man who was allegedly left ‘beaten up’.

The incident happened at the 21 Club in Cheltenham just hours after Gloucester beat Zebre 69-12 in the European Challenge Cup.

The club released a statement saying it "stands by the pair unreservedly".

The statement read: "Following an accusation regarding Johan Ackermann and Ruan Ackermann we can confirm an incident took place in Cheltenham last weekend.

"Gloucester Rugby are aware that a small number of individuals are posting comments about the incident which are untrue.

"Gloucester Rugby do not condone violence in any shape or form and having reviewed the evidence, the club stands by the pair unreservedly."

Instagram user Katie.c.allen posted photos of a man said to be her brother with facial injuries and a lengthy caption reading: “My brother @harrymallen was violently attacked in bar 21 in Cheltenham on Sunday early morning by professional rugby player @ruanackermann and his Dad the head coach Johan Ackermann, as well as another person who is yet to be identified via CCTV.

“The attack was completely unprovoked, they pulled his jumper over his head, push his head down and took multiple blows to his head, face and arms.

“He is currently in A&E having an X-Ray on his face, and a CT on his head as there is a concern of a internal bleed.

@officialgloucesterrugby are yet to have made any comment, the police have plenty of evidence and no doubt with be arresting these t***s. They shouldn’t be allowed to use their size, fame and power to treat others with such disrespect.

“The media will be posting the story too. I’m blessed to have a platform on instagram, so I’ll make sure I use it to get these guys done!”

The club since released the a statement, and Gloucester Rugby CEO Stephen Vaughan added:"Gloucester Rugby takes any allegations extremely seriously and we have conducted a full investigation in to this matter.

"Having spoken to Johan, Ruan, the club where the alleged incident took place, the police and other independent witnesses as well as viewing subsequent CCTV footage, I am completely satisfied that whilst an incident did take place, it was instigated by others and not by Johan and Ruan as has been incorrectly reported.

"Both Johan and Ruan have been upset and disappointed by the allegations, but do appreciate the support from Gloucester Rugby fans, players, colleagues, friends and family."

A police spokesperson said: “Gloucestershire Constabulary is investigating a report of an incident which happened at the 21 Club in Regent Street, Cheltenham, in the early hours of Sunday morning – December 17.

“The allegation is that there was an altercation between two groups of men, during which one of the men sustained facial injuries. The incident was reported just before 1.30am.

“Police officers arrived shortly afterwards but all those involved had left the scene. Investigating officers will be examining CCTV footage this week and speaking to witnesses. No-one has been arrested."

The 21 Club in Regent Street is a 20-minute drive from Gloucester’s Kingsholm stadium.

The venue’s website says: “If you enjoy a vibrant party atmosphere in a safe and friendly environment, the 21 Club is the place for you.”

The 300-capacity bar is open until 3am and boasts three basements and an outside heated courtyard.
