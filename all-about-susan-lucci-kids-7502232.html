All About Liza and Andreas

Publish date: 2024-01-29

Susan Lucci is used to playing a mom on camera, but she's also a mother to two kids in real life.

The soap opera star played the notorious Erica Kane on All My Children from 1970 to 2011 and went on to star in Hot in Cleveland, Devious Maids and Army Wives.

Before all of her success, though, Lucci married TV producer Helmut Huber in 1969. They met working at a hotel and dated for three weeks before getting engaged. On Sept. 13, 1969, they got married.

Lucci and Helmut welcomed their first child, daughter Liza, in 1975 and their son Andreas followed over a decade later in 1988. The pair raised their children in Garden City, New York, which Lucci has said provided a sense of calm to their otherwise hectic lives.

Over the years, the actress has taken her kids to Hollywood events, supported their charity projects and celebrated them on their respective wedding days. The family was also there for one another when Huber died at age 84 in March 2022.

In an interview with Women's Wear Daily in 2018, Lucci said that she was a very involved parent, despite the demands of her job. She told the outlet that she took Liza to dance classes, riding lessons and equestrian competitions. The actress even said she drove carpools and planned family meals.

Here's everything to know about Susan Lucci's kids.

Liza Huber, 48

Liza Huber, Lucci and Helmut's first child, was born on Feb. 22, 1975, in Long Island, New York.

Lucci's elder child graduated from the University of North Carolina Chapel Hill with a degree in communication and media studies and went on to study acting at the Terry Schreiber Studio in New York City. Following in her mother's footsteps, Liza portrayed Gwen Hotchkiss Winthrop in the soap opera Passions from 1999 to 2008.

In the middle of her time on the series, on March 13, 2004, Liza married businessman Alexander Hesterberg III, per The New York Times. The couple, who met each other in elementary school, reconnected at a concert in Manhattan and got married two years later. They now have four children of their own: Royce, Brendan, Hayden and Mason.

Lucci has said she adores being a grandmother. "I love the hugs and kisses!" she told Closer Weekly in 2016. "And Hayden running to me in her tutu shouting, 'Grammy! Grammy!' This warmth from the children is the best."

In 2012, Liza stepped away from acting and started the baby food brand Sage Spoonfuls, after not being able to find homemade meals she trusted for her kids. She was featured in Forbes' "Female Entrepreneurs Rocking the World" series in 2015 and was a parent ambassador for Healthy Child Healthy World, per her bio on the Sage Spoonfuls' website.

Liza and Lucci have also been involved in ADAPT Community Network, formerly known as United Cerebral Palsy of New York City, for very personal reasons. Liza's son Brendan was diagnosed with the neurological disorder in 2010.
