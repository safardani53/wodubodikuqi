Von Miller: Professional Career

Early Career

Born in an athletic family, Von was interested in sports from the early days. After joining the DeSoto High School in DeSoto, Texas, he joined the school football team and started playing for the team.

During his high school days, he was also named the District 8-5A Defensive MVP.

Upon completing his schooling days, he joined Texas A&M University and played for Texas A&M Aggies football, where he played from 2007-to 2010.

As a junior, he played as a defensive end/linebacker, which allowed him to utilize his pass-rushing abilities. Likewise, he was also named a first-team All-American.

During his senior season at the University, he started playing as a 3–4 outside linebacker under Tim DeRuyter’s defense. Sadly Von suffered from a high ankle sprain that led him to miss six games.

After returning from the injury, he was able to win the Butkus Award as the nation’s top linebacker. Upon completing his degree in poultry science, he graduated from the university.

Professional Career

Miller started his professional career in football in the 2010 NFL Draft. He was able to rank 2nd in the 40-yard dash, 3rd in the vertical jump, 1st in the broad jump, and 3rd in the 20-yard shuttle.

After that, Von was drafted to Denver Broncos professional football team. He joined the team in 2011.

During the game for the 2011 season, he was selected for the 2012 Super Bowl and also named the AP Defensive Rookie of the Year.

At the beginning of the 2012 season, he registered 10 sacks in 9 games. Sadly after hitting quarterback Cam Newton, he was fined $21,000 on November 16, 2012.   

After violating the game policy, Miller was suspended for four games in the 2013 season. Likewise, returning from the ACL injury in 2014, Von continued his fourth season with the Broncos.

Moreover, he became the third-fastest player in NFL history on September 17, 2015. He finished his 2015 season with 11 sacks, 35 total tackles, 32 quarterback hits, one pass defense, and four forced fumbles.          

After extending the contract with the Broncos, he became the highest-paid defensive player in NFL history on March 1, 2016.

He recorded his fourth consecutive season with at least ten sacks for the 2017 season with the Broncos. Similarly, he continued the season with the Denver Broncos until 2021.

After Broncos, he was traded to Los Angeles Rams on November 1, 2021. He made his debut with the Los Angeles team on Monday Night Football, losing to the San Francisco 49ers.

For the 2021 season, he recorded 50 tackles, 9.5 sacks, one pass defended, and one forced fumble in 15 games played.
