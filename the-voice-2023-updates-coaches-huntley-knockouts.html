The Voice 2023 LIVE Fans in uproar after Niall Horan sends singer packing following 'outstanding

Publish date: 2024-07-06

NIALL faced a tough decision on who to let go on the latest episode of The Voice - and fan's weren't happy with the choice he made.

John Legend was left sobbing after a performance by Team Niall's Lennon VanderDoes in the singer's knockout against Alexa Wildish and Julia Roome.

"I'm so upset with you, Niall," Legend said with tears streaming down his face. The coach was furious as Niall put three great singers together - meaning two of them had to go home as there were no steals or saves left.

Gwen Stefani also noted that she had tears in her eyes before Reba McEntire got up from her chair and handed Legend a tissue.

Niall chose Alexa to continue in the competition, sending Lennon and Julia home, outraging fans.

"He was just breathtaking," one fan said. "Niall made a mistake letting him go."

Another wrote: "I thought you were outstanding with your performance."

Tune in to The Voice next Monday at 8 pm ET on NBC tonight to see which singers return for the playoffs. Fans can also catch new episodes on Peacock, Fubo, Sling TV and Hulu Live TV.

  • The grand prize

    The winner of The Voice reportedly receives $100,000 as well as a record deal with Universal Music Group. 

    In some cases, the winner’s celebrity coach offers additional perks. 

    Former coach Blake Shelton has allowed winners from his team to tour with him, and he has pushed Universal Music Group to properly promote their records.

  • All about The Voice host

    Carson Daly is a television host, radio broadcaster, producer, and television personality from the United States. 

    He worked as a VJ on MTV’s Total Request Live and as a DJ for the Southern California radio station 106.7 KROQ-FM from 1998 until 2003.

    Currently, he is heavily involved with NBC where he has joined its morning show, Today, and is the host of The Voice.

  • Kelly moves coasts

    While no official reason has been given for her decision to not return as a coach for season 24, Variety has claimed that Kelly Clarkson’s daytime TV show The Kelly Clarkson Show will move to New York City from Los Angeles—where The Voice tapes.

    During an appearance on TalkShopLive, Kelly explained that her loved ones inspired the decision to change the soundstage. 

    She was adamant about moving, even if it meant canceling her chat program. NBC agreed to her request.

    “Being isolated and so far from your family, that’s not good for anyone. My family is East Coast, they’re North Carolina based,” she said to the outlet. “So, it was one of those things where I just had to [do it].”
