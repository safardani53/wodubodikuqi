Madonna was secretly recording music with pop superstar just hours before hospital dash

Publish date: 2024-09-16

IT wasn’t just tour rehearsals which had Madonna feeling exhausted ahead of her collapse last weekend.

I can reveal that just hours before being found unresponsive in her New York home, the Like A Prayer singer was locked in the studio with fellow pop superstar Katy Perry.

Madge is reported to have felt under the weather in the run-up to her shock blackout but she was determined to push on with her gruelling schedule.

This included punishing 12-hour days rehearsing at Nassau Coliseum in Long Island ahead of her 84-date Celebration tour, which has now been postponed.

The superstar had also been working with Katy on a special project as part of her celebrations to mark her 40 years in the business.

A source said: “Madonna has long admired Katy and they have worked together before but never managed to actually put anything out.

“Madonna has been working around the clock to make sure both her music and her world tour are second to none.

“Obviously everything has been put on hold now but the fact she was in the studio with Katy just hours before being rushed to the ICU shows how determined she was to push ahead despite feeling unwell.”

On the same day, Madonna was also spotted visiting New York’s Metropolitan Museum of Art, which is the location for the world-famous A-lister black-tie fundriaser, the Met Gala.

Work on ice

The music icon is said to have been suffering with illness for a month but refused to cut down her schedule as she was determined to make sure everything was perfect.

Madonna’s Celebration Tour was due to kick off in Vancouver, Canada in a fortnight’s time.

The trek included five sold-out nights at London’s O2 Arena in October and December.

However, earlier this week her manager confirmed all work commitments are now on ice while Madonna recovers.

The Material Girl was raced to the hospital last Saturday after developing a serious bacterial infection.

Her manager Guy Oseary, who has worked with the superstar for decades, said: “Her health is improving, however she is still under medical care. A full recovery is expected.”

She is now understood to have been discharged and is back at her New York home but is under the watchful eyes of doctors.

Godspeed, Madge.

Biffy: Without support we'd be just like Lew

BIFFY Clyro have sympathised with their fellow Scotsman Lewis Capaldi, admitting they would have had to take a break for their mental health if they didn’t have each other’s help.

The group, consisting of frontman Simon Neil and twins James and Ben Johnston, have been together for almost three decades and they said there have been tough times, just like those which the Someone You Loved singer is experiencing now.

Lewis has cancelled his shows for the foreseeable future to look after himself.

In an exclusive chat at the O2 Silver Clef Awards in London yesterday, Simon said: “You need a lot of stamina when you’re going on the road.

And we’re lucky that I guess as a band we’ve got each other.

“That can give you a support network that perhaps can stave off those moments of panic.

“Someone like Lewis, he’s experiencing it all himself – the pressure to be charming, funny, sing amazing. And he’s obviously struggling. We’ve been close to those points, but we’ve always been able to rejuvenate and kind of come back. But that’s because we have support from each other.”

They won the Best Live Act gong at the ceremony, which was raising money for the music therapy charity Nordoff Robbins, while other winners included Wet Leg for the New Music Award and Nova Twins with the Innovation Award.

Neneh Cherry was the recipient of the Outstanding Achievement award, but the Swedish singer has ruled out any involvement in Eurovision next year.

Asked about the possibility, she said: “No, no, no, no.” I’ll take that as a no, then.

Stormzy took home the flagship O2 Silver Clef Award.

But it was lucky he was there in time to collect it, given he turned up at 3.30pm – four hours after guests were told to arrive for the red carpet.

Perhaps he should celebrate his achievement by buying a watch.
