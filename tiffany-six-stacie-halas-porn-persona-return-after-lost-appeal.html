Will Stacie Halas' Porn Persona Return After Lost Appeal?

Publish date: 2024-02-11

Will Stacie Halas, whose teaching career was terminated because of her brief porn star past as Tiffany Six, return to adult films after failing to get her job back?

Dubbed the “porn star teacher,” Halas lost her appeal this week, failing to convince a judge to reverse her dismissal and that you can “overcome your past.”

Her lawyer added, in vain, “I think she’s representative of a lot of people who have a past that may not involve anything illegal or anything that hurts anybody.”

Judge Julie Cabos-Owens disagreed.

 

“Although her pornography career has concluded,” she wrote, “the ongoing availability of her pornographic materials on the internet will continue to impede her.”

Halas’ right to engage in legal activity was not infringed upon, but neither was the school firing her if she can’t be an “effective teacher and respected colleague.”

So what will the former Tiffany Six do now?

Her bid to return to middle school science teaching in Oxnard, Calif., was noble, but like it or not, it’s been dashed, leaving her out of a job … but not opportunity.

Despite the obvious stereotypes now associated with Halas, she could use this as a force for good in an age where everything is put on Facebook and YouTube.

As a prime example of how long term effects may be rendered by short term decisions, her example may be just what teenagers today need most.

Of course, if Stacie Halas decides to take another, divergent path in life, well, at least she already has a different field she was already successful in.

The potential for porn titles spoofing her legal drama is endless.
