SISTER Wives star Aspyn Brown and her husband, Mitch Thompson, have dropped $445,000 on a Utah home near her mom Christine amid her parents’ nasty split. 

The U.S. Sun can exclusively confirm Aspyn, 27, and her husband, Mitch, purchased a $445,000 home in Midvale, Utah on December 15, 2022. 

The 1,272 square-foot home boasts three bedrooms and two bathrooms, according to Utah property records. 

The home features a fireplace, finished basement, garage and has red brick exterior. 

Mitch expressed his excitement over the purchase, as he captioned an Instagram selfie of the couple in front of their new abode: “We bought our first home! Just in time for Christmas.”

The property is just a 20-minute drive from Aspyn’s mom Christine’s Utah home.

Dad Kody, who split from Christine in 2021, expressed his desire to have his children move near him and settle down in Flagstaff, Arizona.

Kody, 54, had four wives Meri, 51, Christine, 50, Janelle, 53, and Robyn, 44, when he purchased land called Coyote Pass for $820,000 in 2018.

He planned to build four homes on the property for each wife, but his separations from Janelle and Meri were announced late last year.

Aspyn and Mitch have lived in Utah and ultimately chose to settle down closer to Christine.

The couple got married in 2018 in a wedding ceremony that was filmed for Sister Wives. 

Christine moved into a $1.1million Utah duplex after her split from Kody.

Christine and Kody’s children Mykelti and Paedon also live in Utah, as well as their youngest daughter, Truley, who still lives at home.

Ysabel attends college in North Carolina, while Gwendlyn is enrolled in a university in Flagstaff.

NASTY SPLIT

Kody and Christine’s breakup has been nasty, as the patriarch was enraged last season when his ex moved Truley, 12, with her to Utah. 

During the Sister Wives three-part Tell All on Sunday, Kody and Christine continued to argue.

The plural husband accused Christine of “s**t talking” Meri and Janelle while talking with host Sukanya Krishanan in one conversation.

But sitting alone with Sukanya, Christine hit back: “He would complain about the other wives too!”

She added of Robyn, who fans have dubbed his “favorite”: “But not Robyn. He would never complain about Robyn.”

In another scene, Kody explained to Sukanya: "Initially my issue was I thought Christine was playing a game and so I just went ambivalent, I was trying to be stoic about it."

"Why did you think she was playing a game?" Sukanya then asked, as Kody simply replied: "Cause she's a game player."

"Help me understand that," Sukanya probed, as Kody claimed: "From the time we were married, manipulation, pouting, you know temper tantrums, just behavior things.”

Sukanya then asked: "Was Christine playing you and playing the game? Because it seems very, very cold, like a cold thing to say, not something that a husband would say to his wife.”

Kody responded: "Some of the games that were happening, were things like when we moved to Flagstaff, Arizona, I was trying to reconcile with Meri.

"And Meri gave me this really cool birthday present, and I'm in this place expressing to Janelle, Christine and Robyn that I might reconcile with Meri, and Christine lost her… that day.

"She comes to a family party and leaves yelling to one of the kids that she was in a loveless marriage. This is two years before she kicks me out.”

"Why that reaction from Christine?" Sukanya said, as Kody replied: "I was trying to reconcile with Meri, 'cause there's something wrong with in the relationship between Meri and Christine."

Christine responded to the accusations: “Why would I be offended to a marriage that's broken like that?

"We were fighting for so long, we've been having the same conversation for forever and ever and about time, all of that, and he kept saying, 'stop comparing, stop comparing' and I'm like, 'dude, I don't think I'm the one that's comparing here.'"

Sukanya then pointed out: "He called it poison, was he saying you're the poison or was he comparing the relationship to being poison?"
