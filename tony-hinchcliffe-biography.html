Tony Hinchcliffe’s career and net worth

Hinchcliffe relocated to Los Angeles in 2007 to pursue a career in comedy, appearing at The Comedy Store in West Hollywood. He eventually became a regular, opening for comedians Joe Rogan and Jeff Ross. 

He mocked and roasted fellow comics and handled delicate themes in his caustic approach. Ross coached him and assisted him in landing writing gigs for Comedy Central Roasts.

Hinchcliffe wrote for James Franco, Justin Bieber, and Rob Lowe roasts. He also wrote for The Burn with Jeff Ross and Jeff Ross Presents Roast Battle.

Aside from his stand-up career, Hinchcliffe is well-known for his podcast, Kill Tony, which he has been hosting since 2013. He and his panel of visitors give live critique and hilarious commentary, giving up-and-coming comedians a platform to display their ability.

He mocked and roasted Snoop Dogg in 2016 and established The Pony Hour podcast. One Shot, his one-hour stand-up special, is available on Netflix. In 2017, he headlined the Monster Energy Outbreak Tour.

In 2020, he relocated to Austin, Texas, where he joined Rogan and relocated Kill Tony to several locations, most recently The Comedy Mothership, Joe Rogan’s comedy club.

Hinchcliffe’s distinct comic style frequently incorporates sarcastic comments on many parts of society, with an emphasis on current events and pop culture. His razor-sharp style and ability to captivate audiences with challenging topics have won him a cult following.

Hinchcliffe has appeared on several comedy specials, television shows, and podcasts during his career, demonstrating his flexibility as a comedian and performer. His humor and gutsy approach to comedy have earned him critical recognition as well as financial success in the comedy industry, elevating him to a prominent figure in the stand-up comedy scene.

Tony Hinchcliffe getting canceled

In 2021, Hinchcliffe was canceled for using racial slurs against fellow comedian, Peng Dang.

In May 2021, Hinchcliffe was caught on film disrespecting Dang, an Asian American comedian who had introduced Hinchcliffe following his earlier show at Austin’s Vulcan Gas Company. 

Hinchcliffe called Dang various racial slurs and then launched into a series of Asian clichés in a mimic Chinese accent, labeling audience members who smiled at Dang’s jokes as “race traitors.” 

The video was eventually shared on Twitter by Dang, where it quickly went viral and people demanded Hinchcliffe get canceled.

As a consequence, Hinchcliffe’s agency WME dropped him, he was pulled from Rogan’s gigs in Austin, and the Austin nightclub Antones declared that it would no longer be connected with Hinchcliffe or his Kill Tony live act.

According to Vulture, during his show, Hinchcliffe suggested that Chinese people traveled to Austin for the bats and chastised angry audience members for believing he was serious. Dang reported that he did not get an apology from Hinchcliffe as a result of the event.
