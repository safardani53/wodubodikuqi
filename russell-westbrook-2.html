Russell Westbrook | Early Life, Family, & Education

Russell Westbrook III was born in Long Beach, California, United States. His parents are Russell Westbrook Jr. and Shannon Horton.

His father is a former basketball player. Although he never played professionally, he was well-known for his college career.

Russell’s dad was very athletic and played other sports too.

The Westbrook family didn’t have much growing up, but they made the best out of everything.

Russell’s parents always inspired him to be the best at whatever he did.

They supported his career choice and encouraged him to pursue basketball.

The NBA player also has a brother named Raynard Westbrook.

He played college-level football at the University of Central Oklahoma. The brothers are very close and bonded over their love for sports.

The point guard attended a public high school called Leuzinger High School. He had an exceptional high school career with the Leuzinger Olympians.

He was offered a scholarship to join the UCLA Bruins. However, he didn’t finish college. He entered the NBA Draft after his sophomore year.

NBA Career

Oklahoma City Thunder

The Thunders selected Westbrook in the first round as the fourth pick overall. In his first season, he was on the NBA All-Rookie First Team. He also assisted the Oklahoma team in reaching the playoffs in his second season.

Then in 2011, he was selected for the NBA All-Star Game. He was on the All-NBA Second Team. He was again picked for the 2012 NBA All-Star Game.

Westbrook helped the Thunders reach the NBA finals that year. However, they lost to the Miami Heat.

Russell helped Oklahoma City to qualify for the playoffs again. They were also the first seed of the Western Conference.

Westbrook suffered an injury later that year which put him out for the rest of the 2013 season. Thunders were eventually eliminated, but the point guard became a three-time All-NBA Second Team.

He was selected for the 2015 All-Star game, where he was the All-Star MVP. He became the MVP for the second time in 2016.

The former Bruins even started the 2016 All-Star game.

He signed an $85.7 million contract extension for three years with the Thunders.

The father of three won the 2017 NBA Most Valuable Player Award. Besides that, he was well-known for signing the largest contract in NBA history the same year. His final season with the Oklahoma City Thunders was the 2018-19 season.

Houston Rockets

For the 2019-20 season, Westbrook was traded to the Rockets in exchange for Chris Paul. He got to play with James Harden on the Houston team again.

The former UCLA player played great until the pandemic resulted in the cancellation of the season. Furthermore, he tested positive for Covid-19.

He recovered later and joined the Rockets for the rest of the league at Disney World in Orlando.

Russell failed to achieve the level of success he had with the Thunders. The season’s records were Russell’s worst postseason career records.

Washington Wizards

After much speculation, the point guard left the Rockets. Initially, many fans linked his request for a trade to his poor performance. Many rumors suggested otherwise.

Several media outlets reported some tensions between Westbrook and Harden resulted in his trade.

Los Angeles Lakers

On August 6, 2021, Russell joined the Lakers, and this is the third time he was traded in three years.

2022 

For the 2022- 2023 season, Westbrook earned a base salary of $47,063,478 while carrying a cap hit of $47,063,478 and a dead cap value of $47,063,478 after signing by the Los Angeles Lakers.

On August 1, 2022, Russell changed his agent, Thad Foucher of Wasserman, to Jeff Schwartz of Excel Sports for his representation.

On October 28, 2022, Westbrook chipped in 18 points and 8 rebounds in a 111–102 loss to the Minnesota Timberwolves after his rookie season as he came off the bench.

Later with 23,335 points on a second-quarter free throw, he passed Robert Parish for 29th on the all-time scoring list.

In December 2022, Westbrook recorded his first triple-double off the bench in a 133–122 overtime loss to the Philadelphia 76ers.

Again, Westbrook put up a triple-double with 15 points, 11 rebounds, and 12 assists off the bench in a 128–106 win over the Denver Nuggets.

Not only that, but in Lakers history, he became the first player to put up multiple triple-doubles off the bench.
