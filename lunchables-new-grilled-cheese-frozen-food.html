Lunchables introduces two new flavors as kids get a warm take on a cheesy classic

Publish date: 2024-03-09

LUNCHABLES have moved beyond cheese and crackers to introduce a first-of-its-kind snack pack.

The company is heating things up with the new crispy Grilled Cheesies.

Sold in the frozen aisle, the grilled cheese sandwiches have to be popped in the microwave for 60 seconds before being enjoyed.

They feature specially designed "360CRISP" packaging to achieve the perfect golden-brown crunch on the bread without needing to break out pans or turn on the stove.

There are two flavors; Original, which features Kraft singles, and Pepperoni Pizza which has the same melty cheese and pepperoni kids already love from other Lunchables snack packs.

Kraft Heinz hopes to "revolutionize the microwave" with its first expansion into the frozen food sections.

read more on new food

“The launch of 360CRISP™ is a clear proof point in our mission to reinvent the frozen category by providing high-quality, delicious, and convenient solutions for snack-time and beyond,” said Alan Kleinerman, Vice President Head of Disruption at Kraft Heinz, in a press release.

“At Kraft Heinz, we’re sitting at the intersection of food and technology – building consumer-first innovations that revolutionize the meal table and importantly, drive real value for families around the world. 360CRISP™ is just the beginning.”

Shoppers will be able to find them in stores beginning this month for $4.99 per box of two sandwiches.
