Early Life & Family

Davon Reed was born to David Reed and Rose Reed on June 11, 1995, in Ewing Township, New Jersey. He grew up with a younger brother named Miles.

Basketball has been a part of Davon’s life from a very young age, as his father, David, introduced him to the sport.

His father purchased a 10 feet court when he was only five years old and began participating in organized basketball at a young age.

Reed was able to score his first dunk before starting his freshman year of high school during a summer league game.

High School & Collegiate Basketball

Davon Reed attended his high school at Princeton Day School from 2019 to 2013.

With a total of 2102 points, Reed established himself as the all-time leading scorer in his high school’s history.

Reed committed to play collegiate basketball for Miami over offers from Wake Forest, Cincinnati, Xavier, and Memphis.

Throughout his four-year college career, he averaged 10.3 points and 3.6 rebounds per game.

Career

The Phoenix Suns selected Davon Reed as the 32nd overall pick in the 2017 NBA draft on June 22, 2017.

However, due to a left meniscus injury that required surgery, he made his NBA debut on January 14, 2018, playing for five minutes against the Indiana Pacers.

During the 2017-18 season, Reed appeared in 21 games, starting in one, before the Suns waived him and signed Jamal Crawford.

Reed joined the Indiana Pacers for the 2018-19 season, but an injury cut his season short after playing only 10 games.

In the 2019-20 season, he played for the Sioux Falls Skyforce, the G League affiliate of the Miami Heat, and averaged 12.7 points, 6.6 rebounds, and 3.8 assists per game.

For the 2020-21 season, Reed joined the Taoyuan Pilots of the P. League+ before joining the Denver Nuggets for the 2021-22 season.

In his first year with the Nuggets, he played in 48 games, starting in five, and averaged 4.4 points per game.

In the 2022-23 season, he played 35 games for the Nuggets, averaging 2.3 points per game before being traded to the Los Angeles Lakers in the mid-season in a four-team trade.

Reed’s career high of 17 points came on March 7, 2022, in a game for the Nuggets against the Golden State Warriors.

Net Worth & Contracts

Contracts

An NBA draft pick of the Phoenix Suns, Reed signed with them on a four-year contract worth $5.57 million. It averaged an annual salary of $1.39 million & guaranteed $1.5 million.

However, the Suns waived him on October 16, 2018.

The very next day, Reed signed a two-way contract with the Indiana Pacers and their G League affiliate, the Fort Wayne Mad Ants. Indiana waived their free-agent exception rights on July 6, 2019.

Reed then signed a contract with the Miami Heat on September 4, 2019, using the Exhibit 10 clause. However, he was waived by the Heat on October 19, 2019.

On September 27, 2021, he signed a contract with the Denver Nuggets using the Exhibit 10 clause but was waived by the team on October 16, 2021.

He then signed three 10-day hardship contracts with the Nuggets on December 4, 19, and 20 of 2021, worth $95,930, $94,304, and $95,930, respectively.

Then, on January 9, 2022, he signed a two-way contract with the Nuggets and Grand Rapids Gold.

Denver extended a two-way qualifying offer worth $1.58 million on June 27, 2022, making him a restricted free agent.

On July 9, 2022, he agreed to a two-year contract worth $3.97 million with the Nuggets, which had an annual average salary of $1.98 million and a guaranteed amount of $1.9 million.

However, on February 9, 2023, Reed was traded to the Los Angeles Lakers as part of a four-team trade.

Following the end of the 2023-24 season, he will be an unrestricted free agent.
