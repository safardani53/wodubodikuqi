Anger as London primary school gives parents just three days' notice of religious holiday closure

Publish date: 2024-05-15

PARENTS are furious after a school announced it will be closed on Monday so staff can celebrate the Muslim festival of Eid-al-Adha - giving them only three days notice.

Chobham Academy, in Newham, east London, said its primary school would be closed due to a “high number of staff requesting leave for religious observance.”

Head Mark Elms offered his "sincere apologies" for the closure but parents have still been left outraged.

Mr Elms was a "super head" at his old school, where he was thought to be Britain's highest paid headteacher with a staggering £276,000 salary.

One mum, who did not want to be named, said: “It’s outrageous that they have done this at such short notice."

“We wouldn’t mind if it was included in the school calendar so we all knew well in advance that this was happening.
“But to suddenly spring it on us just three days before is unacceptable.

“People have jobs and lives and will find it difficult to get childcare at such short notice.

“I got a text out of the blue at about 11am on Friday saying the school would be closed on Monday but it didn’t explain why.

“Then they sent the kids home with a letter from the head saying it was because a lot of teachers had suddenly asked for the day off.

“I wouldn’t be able to do that in my job. It’s very disruptive.”

Eid-al-Adha is the holiest celebration in the Islamic calendar but is based on the lunar cycle and falls on different dates each year like Easter.

It was expected to fall on September 11 and cause controversy as it’s the 15th anniversary of the 9/11 terror attacks.

But religious leaders in Saudi Arabia announced last Friday that it would in fact fall on September 12.

Chobham Academy has around 1,800 pupils from primary age through to sixth formers.

Head teacher Mark Elms said in a letter to parents that the school would only be closed for nursery, reception, year 1 and year 2 pupils.

He added: “Please accept my sincere apologies for the short notice which could not be helped and for the disruption to the beginning of the new academic year.”

The school said it had no further comment to make.

Chobham Academy opened in 2013 in buildings first used for the London Olympics in 2012.
It has a high proportion of children from ethnic minorities.

An Ofsted inspection rated it “outstanding” last year.
