Duggar fans 'offended' by 'gross' ingredients Joy-Anna uses in new recipe for family dinner in lates

Publish date: 2024-06-29

JOY-Anna Duggar has riled up fans by making dinner from a recipe for beef stroganoff that uses two types of canned soup.

The Counting On alum took to her Instagram Story to share what she had planned for her family's "supper."

Joy-Anna, 25, showed off the ingredients she readied for the meal, including beef, egg noodles, onions, Worcestershire sauce, garlic, sour cream, salt, and pepper.

There were also two cans of Campbell's soup on the counter meant to be used in the recipe: cream of mushroom and cream of chicken.

Fans took issue with the fact that Joy-Anna was using these ingredients, calling them "gross" and calling her recipe into question.

Many questioned the validity of the cookbook in which the recipe was found, given its reliance on processed and canned ingredients.

"I make beef stroganoff for my family of four at least once every couple weeks," one wrote on an online thread. "Never have I ever used cream of crap soup in it. Never. I’m offended that anyone would."

Another explained: "For anyone that wants actually good tasting authentic beef stroganoff, you do not put canned anything in it.

"You make the sauce with beef broth, use real mushrooms, a little flour to thicken, and actual thin cut pieces of steak, not ground beef."

"Who wants to bet she uses condensed milk instead of the sour cream, like Jingle the Kitchen Elf did," another said, using a joking nickname for Joy-Anna's sister, 28-year-old Jinger.

"She needs to throw this cookbook away," concluded one user.

JINGER AILS

Cooking mishaps run in the Duggar family, as evidenced by Jinger's "stinky mistake".

"Check that out. That is ramen with no water, which I used to do all the time as a kid. I ate a lot of Ramen growing up, and no water.

"And so our house stinks so bad I had to air it out for a while. Windows, front door open, everything."
