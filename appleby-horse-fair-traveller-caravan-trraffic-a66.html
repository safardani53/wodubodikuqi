Moment travellers plod along A66 to Appleby Horse Fair with TWO-MILE traffic jam behind them

Publish date: 2024-07-18

THIS is the moment fed up motorists are caught stuck in a TWO MILE traffic jam as travellers plod along with their horses to the Appleby Horse Fair.

A driver travelling in the opposite direction - and probably thankful to be doing so - captured the determined travellers walking their steeds and traditional caravans along the A66.

The two caravans are moving slowly as furious cars, lorries and vans have built up behind them.

The dashcam continues to film the winding line of traffic for miles, as the pair heading to the annual fair cause mayhem.

They seem unconcerned that they are walking very slowly along the main road, and don't appear bothered by the huge queue of traffic at their rear.

An estimated 30,000 visitors are expected to descend on the small Cumbrian town of Appleby over the coming days - the fair officially begins tomorrow before coming to a close on Friday.

The annual event is where where members of the travelling community congregate to show off their horses and trade them.

The earliest record of the fair is in a charter from Henry II, dating from the 12th century but it is believed to have a longer tradition than that.

It marks the RSPCA’s biggest deployment of staff of the year with 31 officers attending alongside representatives from a number of equine charities.

A tradition that was banned last year due to bad weather made a return as travellers today took their horses for a dip in the River Eden to cool them down.

The 17th century ritual began as a way of washing the horses to make them more attractive to potential purchasers.
