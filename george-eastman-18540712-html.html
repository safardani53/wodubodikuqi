George Eastman is an American businessman who founded the Eastman Kodak company and helped make the use of roll film in photography a popular trend. The film was also the basis for the invention of the film warehouse in 1888 by the world's first filmmakers Eadweard Muybridge and Louis Le Prince, and a few years later by their followers Léon Bouly., William Kennedy Dickson, Thomas Edison , The Lumière Brothers and Georges Méliès. He is a major philanthropist, founder of the Eastman School of Music and the schools of dentistry and medicine at the University of Rochester and London Eastman Dental Hospital; contribute to the Rochester Institute of Technology and construct several buildings on the second Massachusetts Institute of Technology facility on the Charles River. In addition, he made large donations to Tuskegee University and Hampton University, historic black universities in the south.
