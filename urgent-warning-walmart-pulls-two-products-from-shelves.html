Urgent warning as Walmart pulls these two products from shelves around the country

Publish date: 2024-08-26

MIXED nuts and a salad dressing have both been recalled from Walmart locations all over the country.

Following recalls of Capri Sun and Home Run Inn Chicago's Frozen Pizza, Walmart is removing two more products from shelves.

On August 24, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) announced recalls for the Great Value four-ounce bags of chopped walnuts and the Mighty Sesame Co. 10.9-ounce squeezable bottles of organic tahini.

Both products were sent to stores around the US, prompting a massive recall.

South Georgia Pecan Co.

The South Georgia Pecan Co. recalled resealable bags of chopped walnuts after finding some had been mislabeled and actually contains pecans.

Read more on recalls

The FDA said this is extremely dangerous as "people who have an allergy or severe sensitivity to pecans run the risk of serious or life-threatening allergic reaction if they consume this product."

No illnesses have been reported to date.

The mislabeled bags were distributed to Walmart stores in Florida, North Carolina, and South Carolina, between the dates of August 8, 2022, and August 16, 2020.

The affected products were sold in resealable bags.

Rushdi Food Industries

Rushdi Food Industries recalled its Mighty Sesame Co. Organic Tahini due to the possibility of Salmonella contamination.

The affected bottles have an expiration date March, 28, 2023, and a Universal Product Code (UPC) of 858313006208.

Bottles were shipped to New York, New Jersey, and Connecticut as well as Walmart stores nationwide during the first 2 weeks of May 2022. 

The company did say it's unlikely many of these bottles are still available for retail based on when they hit the shelves.

Another recall to be aware of

The FDA said Lyons Magnus, the company behind drinks, like Oatly, is voluntarily recalling a dozen of its products because they can make people sick.

The products are packed in various formats under many different brand names.

They include Oatly and Glucerna brands.

Oatly is used at Starbucks but a spokesperson for Oatly told The Sun: "The product potentially affected is not one that was sold or shipped to Starbucks."

Concerns surrounding the recall include the organism Cronobacter sakazakii.

While infection related to Cronobacter sakazakii is rare, the common symptoms of illness could include fever, vomiting, and urinary tract infection.
