IN the ring, Amir Khan was once compared to the great Muhammad Ali after winning an Olympic silver aged just 17, in Athens all the way back in 2004.

The boxer has since been on I'm A Celebrity and hit the headlines after cheating on his wife...

Who is Amir Khan? What is his background?

Amir Khan was born in Bolton on December 8, 1986.

He grew up in the Greater Manchester town in a British-Pakistani family, who are Muslim by faith.

Khan went to Smithills School and then Bolton Community College. He has one brother, Harry, also a boxer and two sisters.

He began to box aged 11, winning three English schools titles and three junior ABA titles, before winning gold at the 2003 Junior Olympics and gold at the 2004 European Student Championships in Lithuania.

Who is Amir Khan's wife Faryal Makhdoom?

Faryal Makhdoom, 26, is a Pakistani-American who was born in New York.
She is an ex-model and also has a degree in political science and journalism from Rutgers University.

Faryal married Amir Khan in May 2013 and the couple have two children.

Lamaisah was born in 2014 and their daughter Alayna Khan was born on April 24, 2018.

They met after being introduced by a mutual friend, but Makhdoom has had a series of fallings-out with Khan's family, seeming to make fun of the Khans through her social media platforms.

Khan has previously had links to Katie Price, despite the former Page 3 model dating Alex Reid at the time - but the boxer has denied these claims.

Has Amir Khan cheated on his wife Faryal Makhdoom?

Amir Khan slept with beautician Sophia Hammani, 22, on May 10, 2018 - just 17 days after his wife gave birth to their daughter Alayna.

Sophia exclusively told The Sun that she spent the night with the boxer in his hotel room after meeting him in a London club.

The former world champ has been accused of cheating on numerous occasions.

In 2017 tweets from Amir Khan’s Twitter account had claimed he had split from wife Faryal Makhdoom… and accused her of cheating on him with Anthony Joshua.

A tweet on the boxer’s account read: "So me and the wife Faryal have agreed to split. I’m currently in Dubai. Wish her all the best."

He later confirmed on snapchat that it was him tweeting it, after Faryal claimed her and Khan's accounts had been hacked.

What is Amir Khan's net worth?

Amir Khan's net worth is £23million, according to The Sunday Times 2017 rich list.

Khan has made a lot of money from his fights, and reportedly scooped £12m from bouts in 2014 alone.

Obviously, the more a boxer fights, the more money they can make, especially if they can make good money from big-name opponents.

In September 2013, he announced plans to 'make Bolton better' and pledged to invest £5m in the local community, which included opening a makeup shop, run by his wife Faryal.

How did Amir Khan make his breakthrough?

Amir Khan's boxing breakthrough came at the 2004 Olympic Games in Athens where Khan surprised the entire field and became national darling when he boxed his way to Olympic silver at the age of just 17.

Khan only lost 30-22 to Cuba's two-time defending champ Mario Kindelan in the lightweight final - missing out on becoming the youngest boxing Olympic champion since Floyd Patterson in 1952.

What is Amir Khan's professional boxing record?

Amir Khan's pro record is 31 wins and four defeats in 35 fights.

He made his debut against David Bailey in Bolton in July 2005 and knocked him out in the first round.

Khan then went on to score 18 wins as a pro, before he lost to Colombia's Breidis Prescott in the MEN Arena in Manchester, thanks to a knockout.

Looking to get his career back on track, he went on to beat Paulie Malinaggi and Marcos Maidana in an eight-bout winning streak that stretched three years.

But Khan then lost to Lamont Peterson in December 2011, but was reinstated as WBA and IBF light-welterweight champ after the American failed a drugs test.

Seven months later, Khan would go on to lose to Danny Garcia on a technical knock out, but kept fighting on, beating Carlos Molina, Julio Diaz, Luis Collazo, Devon Alexander and Chris Algieri, before going down to Canelo Alvarez in May last year after stepping up to middleweight.

Khan then speculated that he may switch countries to Pakistan, after the Olympics began to accept pro boxers into the competition.

Last year, The Sun exclusively revealed that Khan had been fighting for 13 years with a broken HAND and had pins put into the limb to correct the problem.

Khan went on to compete in I'm A Celebrity...Get Me Out Of Here! in 2017.

He was the seventh campmate to be eliminate and will be remembered for being utterly terrified of snakes and for rowing with Iain Lee.

He has participated in several game shows. These include Countdown, Beat the Star, Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? and Celebrity Juice.
