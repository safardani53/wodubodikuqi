Easyjet flight forced to divert to Amsterdam after Brit went on 'drunken rampage' and 'tried to get

Publish date: 2024-03-17

AN EASYJET flight from Cyprus was forced to divert to Amsterdam when a passenger went on a drunken rampage through the plane.

The British man, in his 50s, became increasingly abusive after the plane took off from Paphos on Wednesday evening, and eventually had to be restrained by a man thought to be an off-duty police officer.

The pilot landed in Amsterdam where the man was arrested by Dutch police, to the relief of anxious passengers who clapped as he was led away.

In footage captured by another flyer, the man – who was dressed in a baseball cap, white hoodie and a camouflage jacket – can be seen being taken away by two Dutch officers.

John Wilson, 20, who filmed the incident, was flying back to the UK with his girlfriend having spent Christmas with his parents who live in Cyprus.

He said: “There was this man getting a little bit rowdy. He didn’t seem just drunk – there seemed to be something else about him like he had taken something.”

“Throughout the flight he got louder and louder and a bit more rowdy.”

“At one point he locked himself in the toilets and he was banging on the door trying to get out and he was convinced the cabin crew had locked him in there to keep him quiet.

“He grabbed one of the crew and an air marshal or an off-duty police man got his badge out to try and detain him and calm him down.”

“He was getting really aggressive but they did manage to calm him down eventually.

“Then there was an announcement that we were going to be making a stop at Amsterdam and that’s when the police came on board and arrested him.

Mr Wilson, who works as a barman in Ockendon, Essex, said they were held on the plane for two hours in Amsterdam before continuing on to Luton.

“It was a bit scary, I’m not going to lie," he added.

“I thought he had a screw loose along with a lot of alcohol and I don’t know if he had taken anything.

“It wouldn’t surprise me to find out he was more than drunk.”

An easyJet spokesman said: "easyJet can confirm that flight EZY2134 from Paphos to London Luton on 29 December diverted to Amsterdam due to a passenger onboard behaving disruptively.

The safety and wellbeing of passengers and crew is always easyJet's priority.

Police met the aircraft on landing and the passenger was arrested.

The aircraft has continued to London Luton.

Whilst such incidents are rare we take them very seriously, do not tolerate abusive or threatening behaviour on-board and always push for prosecution."
