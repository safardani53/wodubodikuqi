Tokyo 2020 Olympics

Lin was looking forward to qualifying for the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. However, due to the coronavirus and the pandemic that led after the Olympics was scheduled for 2021. 

Although Dan is considered one of the greatest badminton players, his skills and energy level remain inconsistent with time and age. Hence, the once world no. 1 ranked player was in the 19th position in 2020. 

Moreover, he lost many games as it became hard to keep up with the young badminton players. Nonetheless, Super Dan was determined to give his best to qualify for the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. 

However, he had to top the rankings of Chen Long and Shi Yuqi, who were in the Top 10, to qualify for the Olympics a third time. Lin said,

“Qualifying for the Tokyo Olympics is my biggest challenge; I will continue to do my best and try to get better results out of competitions.“

Nevertheless, after the pandemic, the two-time gold medalist announced his retirement on 4 July 2020. He cited his pain and injuries as one of the main reasons for his retirement. 

The badminton legend said,

“I have been with the national team from 2000 to 2020, and it is very difficult to say goodbye. But my physical abilities and pain no longer allow me to fight alongside my teammates. After 20 years, I have to say goodbye to the national team.”
